Private Tutoring

Gave Programming and Computer Engineering lessons to first, second and third year students from the University of Alicante. Lesson content was tailored for every student's needs, and varied greatly from student to student.

Taught topics include:

  • Basic programming concepts: If-statements, Loops, Functions, Classes, Inheritance, etc.
  • Math: binary (2s complement), Calculus, Matrix Algebra, Discrete Math and Statistics
  • Built-in data structures: Vectors, Linked Lists, Sets, Dictionaries, Stacks, Queues and Priority Queues
  • Graph algorithms: BFS, DFS, A* search, Dijkstra's, Kruskal's, Beam Search and Iterative Deepening
  • Theory of computation: Finite-State Machines, Context-Free Grammars & Turing Machines
  • Optimization & efficiency: Big-O Notation, Algorithm Analysis, Parallel Processing & Multithreading
  • Competitive Programming: Dynamic Programming, Greedy Algorithms, Divide & Conquer and Branch & Cut
  • Advanced data structures: Heaps, BSTs, AVL trees, Union Finds, Segment Trees, Tries and Graphs
  • Computer Architecture: Memory Management, Pipelining & MASM Assembly (32-bit)

Depending on the student, the lessons were given in C++, Java, Python, or a combination of the three.
